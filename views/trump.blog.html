Applause as Feedback

Visualizing Congressional Feedback

On Feb 28, 2017 President Trump addressed Congress for the first time.

The President's speech touched on a number of themes - Immigration, Education, Healthcare and others.

And throughout the speech - Congress provided direct feedback on these themes through their applause


At MakeItMobile, we've done the hard work of annotating the entire speech.

We've scored each applause and by tieing each one back to the current theme, we gain insight into the themes, or issues, most prized by Congress.


Long story short - we've created an interactive graphic to explore the themes of President Trump's speech and Congress's reaction.


How to Read

At 29% of all Congressional applause, Nationalism was the dominant theme of the night.

The President alluded to this theme on 18 different occassions and Congress replied with 29% of all the applause it delivered that night.

Questions

How are themes chosen?

We made a full faith effort to objectively categorize each quote into a specific theme


How is the score for each applause calculated?

The score for an applause is based on its loudness and its duration. In particular:

score = loudness⁄( duration )1.25

Is this an objective measurement of Congress's reaction to President Trump's speech?

No. While we're confident in our analysis, all work and conclusions documented here are based around a single hour of content. Please consider this as a thought experiment where aesthetics and analysis meet Enjoy!.
